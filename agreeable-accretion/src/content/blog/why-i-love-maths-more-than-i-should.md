Mathematics changed the way I approach engineering. It taught me to care less about memorizing techniques and more about understanding structure.

## The appeal

What I enjoy most is the precision. A good mathematical argument forces you to remove vague thinking. If something is true, there should be a reason. If it fails, there should be a counterexample.

That habit transfers well to programming and systems work. Bugs often come from assumptions that were never stated clearly enough to inspect.

## Where it shows up in practice

I feel the influence of mathematics most strongly when I am:

- analyzing algorithmic tradeoffs
- reasoning about control systems
- debugging unexpected behavior in a distributed or embedded system

In each case, the same mindset helps: define the problem carefully, isolate the variables, and test the logic instead of guessing.

Mathematics is not separate from engineering for me. It is one of the reasons engineering stays interesting.
